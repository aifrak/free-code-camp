Satoru Iwata

The gamer who became president of Nintendo

Satoru Iwata presenting his last video game console, the Nintendo 3DS, existing also in several colors. Nintendo released the Nintendo Wii U the 26th Febraury 2011 in Japan.

Here's a time line of Satoru Iwata's life:

  • 1959 - Borns in Hokkaido Prefecture
  • 1982 - Graduates from the Tokyo Institute of Technology University
  • 1983 to 2000 - Works at HAL Laboratory
  • 1984 to 1986 - Works as programmer on Baloon Fight
  • 1993 - Becomes president of HAL Laboratory
  • 1998 to 1999 - Creates Super Smash Bros.
  • 1999 - Ports Pokemon from Gameboy to Nintendo 64
  • 2000 - Joins Nintendo
  • 2002 - Becomes of Nintendo
  • 2004 - Releases the Nintendo DS
  • 2005 - Begins his GDC 2005 keynote by saying: 'On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer.'
  • 2006 - Releases the Nintendo Wii
  • 2007 - Decides for the 'Blue Ocean' strategy: 'We are battling the indifference of people who have no interest in video games.'
  • 2011 - Releases the Nintendo 3DS
  • 2012 - Releases the Nintendo Wii U
  • 2014 - Announces a 50 percent cut on his own pay
  • 2015 - dies at the age of 55

"Mr. Iwata was a strong leader, a unique figure in the gaming industry and an important part of Nintendo's history. He was a visionary in every sense of the word and we will miss him dearly."


— Creative Director of Nintendo Shigeru Miyamoto
